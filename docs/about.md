# About

I am a mechanical engineer and controls-focused R&D builder who drifted naturally into software because the most interesting systems never stay inside one discipline.

My background combines:

- Mechanical design and prototyping.
- Embedded sensing and hardware bring-up.
- Controls, estimation, and simulation.
- Python-first tooling for experiments, analysis, and visualization.
- Machine learning where it speeds up engineering work rather than replacing judgment.

At the University of Washington and in industry roles, I have worked on robotics, medical hardware, precision assembly, and manufacturing analysis. That work includes a [published patent application](experience.md#patents) (WO2026087546A1) for a system that generates realistic CPR-corrupted ECG signals for defibrillator algorithm development, developed at Philips. The recurring pattern across those roles is straightforward: build a system, instrument it properly, find the bottleneck, and turn the rough prototype into something other people can actually use.

## What I Like Building

- Autonomous vehicle and mobile robot systems where sensing, geometry, and control interact tightly.
- Internal tools that make technical workflows easier for non-specialists.
- Hardware-software loops where data from the real system changes the next design decision.
- Simulators and benchmark pipelines that force different methods to compete on the same ground truth.

## What This Site Is For

This is not meant to be a glossy one-page resume. It is a working portfolio:

- project writeups with enough technical detail to be credible,
- a writing section for ideas that used to sit unfinished in Blogspot drafts,
- a stable place to link code, demos, papers, videos, and experiments,
- and a structure that can keep growing with GitHub rather than getting rebuilt from scratch every few months.
